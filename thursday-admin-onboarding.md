## How the Weekly Cycle Works

Each event runs on a fully automated weekly cycle. The specific days and times for each step are configurable in your event settings — you choose what works best for the Thursday League. Here's the flow:

**Step 1 — Invite Emails:** At the day/time you configure, invite emails go out automatically to every active, subscribed golfer. Each golfer gets a personalized email with three one-tap buttons: "I'm In," "I'm Out," or "Not Sure Yet (ask me again later)." No login required — they just tap and they're done.

**Step 2 — Reminder Emails:** A reminder goes out (day/time configurable) but only to golfers who haven't responded yet or who said "Not Sure." Anyone who already replied "In" or "Out" does not get bothered with a reminder. You can configure up to 3 separate reminder emails if you'd like multiple nudges before the deadline.

**Step 3 — RSVP Cutoff:** At your configured cutoff day/time, self-service RSVP locks. After this point, golfers can no longer change their own response. Only you (as the admin) can make changes — useful for last-minute additions or cancellations.

**Step 4 — Confirmation Emails:** After cutoff, two emails can go out automatically:
1. **Golfer Confirmation** — Sent to all confirmed players with the full roster (first initial + last name format, e.g., "J. Herrera"). Anyone can "reply all" to share tee times, game format, etc.
2. **Pro Shop Detail** (optional) — Sent to the pro shop with full names, contact info, and GHIN numbers for Golf Genius setup. You can toggle this on or off.

**Capacity & Waitlist:** You set a default weekly capacity for your event (e.g., 16 = 4 foursomes, 32 = 8 foursomes). Once capacity is reached, additional "I'm In" responses go to a waitlist ranked by response time. You manually decide who gets pulled off the waitlist when spots open — the system doesn't auto-promote anyone, so you have full control. You can also override capacity for individual weeks.

**Weather Forecasts:** Hyper-localized weather forecasts for the game time window automatically appear on the RSVP page and in invite/reminder/confirmation emails. Golfers see temperature, wind, precipitation chance, UV index, and a "golfability" score. This is on by default.

## What You'll See as Admin

**Admin Dashboard** — Shows your event at a glance: next game date, RSVP counts, and any action items needing attention (pending registrations, open spots, etc.).

**RSVP Management** — For each week, you see the full breakdown: In, Out, Not Sure, No Reply, and Waitlist. After cutoff, you can override any golfer's status (e.g., switch someone from "Out" to "In" if they text you last minute).

**Schedule Management** — A rolling 8-week calendar view. You can toggle any week to "No Game" (e.g., for a club tournament or holiday). When you cancel a game, the system automatically sends a cancellation email to all subscribed golfers with the reason you provide and the next scheduled game date.

**Golfer Directory** — View all golfers subscribed to your event, their status, and profile details. You can approve new registrations, deactivate inactive golfers, and manage subscriptions.

**Custom Emails** — A built-in email composer lets you send targeted messages to specific groups: all "In" golfers, all non-responders, everyone, etc. Pre-built templates are available for common scenarios like "Extra Spots Available" or "Weather Advisory," plus a free-form compose option.

## Optional Enhancements

These features are built and ready to go — they just need to be turned on in your event settings. You can enable any of these at any time:

**1. Playing Partner Preferences**
Golfers can rank up to 10 preferred playing partners (per event). The system uses these preferences when generating suggested foursomes.

**2. Tee Time Preferences**
Golfers can indicate whether they prefer an earlier or later tee time when they RSVP each week. The system factors this into foursome groupings.

**3. Automatic Foursome Grouping**
The platform has a full grouping engine with five methods:
- **Harmony** — Uses playing partner preferences to create foursomes where golfers are paired with people they want to play with.
- **Flight Foursomes** — Groups golfers by similar handicap (best players together, etc.).
- **Balanced Foursomes** — Distributes one golfer from each skill quartile (A, B, C, D) into each group for competitive balance.
- **Flight 2-Person Teams** — Pairs golfers with similar handicaps into 2-person teams, then combines teams into foursomes.
- **Balanced 2-Person Teams** — Pairs the best player with the weakest, second-best with second-weakest, etc. for balanced team competitions.

The grouping results are suggestions — you review and adjust before they go out.

**4. GHIN Handicap Sync**
The system can automatically pull current Handicap Index values from GHIN for all golfers who have a GHIN number on file. Keeps handicaps fresh without golfers having to manually update them. Required if you want to use any of the handicap-based grouping methods above.

**5. Pro Shop Detail Email**
A separate email sent to the pro shop at confirmation time with full golfer details (names, emails, phone numbers, GHIN numbers). Useful if the pro shop needs this for Golf Genius setup or for contacting players directly. Toggled on/off in your email settings.

**6. Guest Requests**
Allows golfers to request to bring a guest for a specific week. You review and approve/deny guest requests after the RSVP cutoff. Guests only fill spots that members haven't claimed.
